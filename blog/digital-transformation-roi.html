Digital Transformation ROI: What to Expect

By Saspire Team • May 5, 2026 • 6 min read

The most common question we hear from Belgian SMEs considering digitalization: "What will my return on investment actually be?" Here are real numbers from our 60+ completed projects.

Typical ROI by Project Type

E-Commerce Webshop (€7,500-€15,000 investment)

  • Break-even: 4-8 months
  • Year 1 ROI: 150-300%
  • Revenue drivers: 24/7 ordering, reduced order errors, cross-selling, new customer acquisition
  • Cost savings: €2,000-5,000/month in reduced admin labor

Catalog Digitalization (€3,000-€8,000 investment)

  • Break-even: 2-4 months
  • Year 1 ROI: 400-800%
  • Revenue drivers: Faster product discovery, SEO traffic, reduced print costs
  • Cost savings: €1,500-3,000/month vs. manual catalog management

Business Automation (€2,000-€5,000 investment)

  • Break-even: 1-3 months
  • Year 1 ROI: 500-1200%
  • Revenue drivers: Faster lead response, fewer missed opportunities
  • Cost savings: €2,000-4,000/month in recovered labor hours

Customer Portal (€10,000-€20,000 investment)

  • Break-even: 6-12 months
  • Year 1 ROI: 100-200%
  • Revenue drivers: Customer retention, self-service reducing support costs
  • Cost savings: €3,000-8,000/month in support labor

The Hidden ROI: What Numbers Don't Show

Beyond direct financial returns, our clients consistently report:

  • Employee satisfaction — Staff freed from repetitive tasks are more engaged
  • Customer experience — Faster response times and self-service improve NPS scores
  • Data-driven decisions — Digital systems provide insights that manual processes never could
  • Scalability — Grow revenue without linearly growing headcount
  • Competitive advantage — First-movers in B2B digitalization capture market share

When Will You See Results?

Based on our project data:

  • Week 1-4: Project kickoff, discovery, and architecture
  • Month 2-3: Development and testing
  • Month 3-4: Launch and initial adoption
  • Month 4-6: Optimization based on real usage data
  • Month 6+: Steady-state ROI with compounding benefits

How to Build Your Business Case

  1. Measure current costs — Track hours spent on manual processes for 2 weeks
  2. Identify revenue leaks — Lost orders, slow response times, missed opportunities
  3. Calculate labor cost — Hours × fully-loaded hourly rate (typically €35-55 for Belgian SMEs)
  4. Project conservative gains — Assume 50% of theoretical savings in year 1
  5. Compare to investment — Most projects pay for themselves within 6 months

The Cost of Doing Nothing

Every month you delay digitalization, you're paying the "manual tax" — the hidden cost of inefficiency. For a typical Belgian SME, this ranges from €3,000 to €10,000 per month in lost productivity, missed sales, and competitive erosion.
